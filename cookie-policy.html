What Are Cookies and Similar Technologies

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit our website. They help us provide you with a better, faster, and safer experience by remembering your preferences, analyzing how you use our platform, and enabling certain functionality.

Similar technologies we use include:

  • Web Beacons: Small graphic images that help us understand user behavior and email engagement
  • Pixels: Tiny code snippets that collect information about your interactions with our content
  • Local Storage: Browser storage that retains data locally on your device
  • Session Storage: Temporary storage that expires when you close your browser
  • Flash Cookies: Data stored by Adobe Flash Player (if installed)
  • Device Fingerprinting: Techniques that identify devices based on their unique characteristics
  • Server Logs: Files that record requests made to our servers

Cookie Duration and Persistence

Cookies have different lifespans depending on their purpose:

Session Cookies

  • Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser
  • Used for essential functions like maintaining your login session
  • Automatically deleted when your browsing session ends
  • Cannot be used to track you across different browsing sessions

Persistent Cookies

  • Remain on your device for a specified period or until manually deleted
  • Remember your preferences and settings between visits
  • Enable personalized experiences and faster website loading
  • Have expiration dates ranging from days to years

Specific Retention Periods

  • Authentication Cookies: 30 days or until logout
  • Preference Cookies: 1 year or until changed by user
  • Analytics Cookies: 2 years (Google Analytics standard)
  • Marketing Cookies: 30-90 days depending on campaign
  • Security Cookies: Session-based or 24 hours
  • Performance Cookies: 1 year for optimization data

Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You have several options for controlling how cookies are used on your device:

Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can typically:

  • View all cookies stored on your device
  • Delete existing cookies individually or all at once
  • Block all cookies from being set
  • Block only third-party cookies
  • Set up notifications when cookies are being placed
  • Create exceptions for specific websites
  • Configure automatic deletion of cookies when closing the browser

Browser-Specific Instructions

  • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and Security > Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Options > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Cookies and site data
  • Opera: Settings > Advanced > Privacy & Security > Cookies

Opt-Out Tools and Resources

You can use these tools to opt out of certain types of tracking:

  • Google Analytics Opt-out: Browser add-on to prevent Google Analytics tracking
  • Network Advertising Initiative: Opt out of interest-based advertising
  • Digital Advertising Alliance: Consumer choice tools for online advertising
  • European Interactive Digital Advertising Alliance: EU-specific opt-out options
  • Your Online Choices: European cookie and behavioral advertising controls

Mobile Device Settings

  • iOS: Settings > Privacy > Tracking > Allow Apps to Request to Track
  • Android: Settings > Privacy > Ads > Opt out of Ads Personalization
  • Reset advertising identifiers to limit cross-app tracking
  • Use private browsing modes for temporary sessions

Impact of Disabling Cookies

While you can disable cookies, doing so may affect your experience on our website:

Essential Functions

  • You may need to log in repeatedly during each visit
  • Your course progress may not be saved properly
  • Payment processing may not function correctly
  • Security features may be compromised
  • Some interactive features may not work as expected

Personalization

  • You'll need to reset your preferences on each visit
  • Content recommendations may be less relevant
  • Language and regional settings won't be remembered
  • Customized learning paths may not be available
  • Video quality and playback preferences won't be saved

Performance

  • Pages may load more slowly without caching
  • We may not be able to optimize your experience
  • Technical issues may be harder to identify and resolve
  • Website performance improvements may not be as effective
